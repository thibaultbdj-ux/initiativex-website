Updated v2.0

  • Members holding IXT at either tier (Explorer free; Patron £30/mo)

IXT Classification

Complete Rewrite
Fundamental Change from v1.0 IXT is no longer classified as a utility token outside the FCA perimeter. As of this version, IXT is a fund-wrapped unit within an OEIC/unit trust structure, regulated under FCA CP25/28. This reclassification has material implications for authorisation, reporting, tax, and consumer protection obligations throughout this document.

3.1 Nature of IXT

IXT (InitiativeX Token) is a fund-wrapped unit — specifically, a unitised interest in the IX Collective Investment Scheme. Each IXT represents a proportional entitlement within the IX fund structure, governed by the fund's instrument of incorporation and managed by IX acting as authorised fund manager.

Core Characteristics

  • Fixed Face Value: £1 per IXT (the 1:1 Rule: 1 IXT = £1 GBP)
  • Floating NAV: Calculated quarterly as Total Distributable Profit ÷ Total IXT in Circulation
  • Fund Structure: OEIC/unit trust — each IXT unit represents a proportional interest in fund assets
  • The IX Ecosystem IS the Growth Trust: Each certified Business NFT is a component of the underlying trust portfolio
  • IXT Allocation: Operator 60% / EBT 20% / Consumer 10% / IX 10%

3.2 What IXT Is Not

ClassificationApplies to IXT?Reason
Utility TokenNOIXT is not a voucher, access right, or platform credit — it is a fund unit with economic rights
Security TokenNOIXT is not a transferable security under MiFID II — it is a unit in an authorised CIS
E-MoneyNOIXT does not store monetary value for the purpose of making payment transactions
Cryptoasset (unregulated)NOIXT is issued within a regulated fund structure under CP25/28 — it sits within the FCA perimeter
Fund-Wrapped Unit (CIS)YESIXT is a unit in an OEIC/unit trust, authorised and regulated under CP25/28 framework

3.3 NAV Calculation Methodology

The IXT NAV is recalculated at the end of each calendar quarter using the following formula:

NAV Formula NAV per IXT = Total Distributable Profit (quarter) ÷ Total IXT in Circulation (end of quarter)

Where Total Distributable Profit = Aggregate certified operator distributable surplus, net of management fees (8% standard / 2% enhanced), carried interest reserve, and operating expenses recognised in the period.

3.4 FCA CP25/28 Alignment

IX is structured to operate within the framework set out in FCA Consultation Paper CP25/28 on fund wrappers. Key structural requirements IX is designed to meet include:

  • Fund manager authorisation under FSMA 2000 and COLL sourcebook
  • OEIC/unit trust instrument of incorporation meeting FCA template requirements
  • Depositary appointment for asset safekeeping
  • Quarterly NAV calculation and publication to unit holders
  • Annual fund accounts prepared to FRS 102 standards
  • Investor disclosure documents (Key Investor Information Document equivalent)
Legal Review Required The CP25/28 framework is at consultation stage as of the date of this document. Final rules may differ from consultation proposals. IX's legal counsel must review the final rules and confirm structural compliance prior to any regulatory filing or investor communication.

FCA Considerations

Rewrite

4.1 Position Change: Within the FCA Perimeter

In contrast to v1.0, which analysed IX's position outside the FCA regulatory perimeter, IX now operates within the perimeter as an authorised fund manager. This is a deliberate structural choice that provides greater legal certainty, investor protection, and long-term institutional credibility.

4.2 Collective Investment Scheme Analysis

Under s235 FSMA 2000, a Collective Investment Scheme exists where participants do not have day-to-day control over management of property and profits or income are pooled. The IX analysis is as follows:

CIS TestIX PositionConclusion
Pooling of contributionsIXT issued against operator receipts and member subscriptions — pooled at fund levelMeets test
Profit/income pooledDistributable profit calculated at fund level across all certified Business NFT operatorsMeets test
No day-to-day control by participantsIndividual IXT holders do not manage fund investments — IX management doesMeets test
ArrangementGoverned by fund instrument, membership agreement, and Business NFT certification frameworkMeets test
CIS Conclusion IX IS a Collective Investment Scheme. However, it is properly authorised as such under FCA CP25/28, structured as an OEIC/unit trust. This is not a regulatory risk — it is the intended and compliant structure.

4.3 Fund Manager Authorisation

IX requires, and is structured to obtain, the following FCA authorisations:

  • Managing a Collective Investment Scheme — Regulated Activity under RAO Art. 51 (as amended)
  • COLL sourcebook compliance — ongoing fund management conduct requirements
  • Depositary appointment — independent depositary for asset safekeeping and oversight
  • Approved person requirements — relevant senior managers subject to SMCR

4.4 Business NFTs as Fund Components

Business NFTs are not securities. They are the on-chain representation of a certified operator's participation as a component of the IX fund portfolio. The Business NFT certification process (Section 15) serves as the fund's due diligence and asset quality assessment framework. Key points:

  • Business NFTs are non-transferable without IX consent (preventing secondary market speculation)
  • Business NFT certification tiers (Foundation / Verified / Excellence) influence distributable profit allocation weighting
  • Decertification constitutes removal of that operator from the fund portfolio
  • Business NFTs do not confer direct ownership rights in operators — they evidence fund component status

4.5 Carried Interest and Management Fee Structure

The IX fee structure is consistent with fund management compensation norms:

  • Management Fee: 8% standard / 2% enhanced — annual fund management fee, deducted prior to NAV calculation
  • Carried Interest: IX share under the 5-stage waterfall (0% / 0% / 10% / 20% / 25% across Stages 0–4) — see Articles of Association v2.1 Article 34
  • Preferred Return: 6% per annum cumulative — Stage 0 founding investors receive this preferentially per venue (ringfenced); generally LPs receive Stage 1 capital return before IX earns Stage 2+ carry
Authorisation Timeline FCA fund manager authorisation is a multi-month process. IX must not represent itself as an authorised fund manager or issue IXT as fund units until formal FCA authorisation is granted. Any pre-authorisation activities must be structured to avoid triggering s19 FSMA 2000 (general prohibition).

Payment Services Regulation

Simplified

5.1 IXT Is Not a Payment Instrument

IXT, as a fund-wrapped unit, falls outside the scope of the Payment Services Regulations 2017 (PSR 2017) and the Electronic Money Regulations 2011 (EMR 2011). The analysis is straightforward:

Regulatory QuestionPosition
Is IXT a payment service under PSR 2017?No. IXT is a fund unit, not a payment transaction instrument. Holding or transferring IXT is a fund operation, not a payment service.
Is IXT e-money under EMR 2011?No. Fund units are expressly excluded from the e-money definition. IXT is not issued against receipt of funds for the purpose of making payment transactions.
Does the Limited Network Exclusion apply?Not relevant. IXT does not require the LNE analysis as it is not a payment instrument at all.
Is IXT redemption a payment service?No. Conversion of IXT to fiat is a fund redemption — a regulated fund activity under COLL, not a payment service under PSR 2017.

5.2 Fund Redemption Mechanics

When a member converts IXT to fiat currency (or applies IXT value against a purchase), this is treated as a fund unit redemption, not a payment transaction. The mechanics are:

  • Redemption requests processed against published quarterly NAV
  • Redemption proceeds paid to member's verified bank account (KYC verified)
  • Redemption frequency subject to fund liquidity provisions in the instrument of incorporation
  • IXT applied at point-of-sale constitutes an in-fund transfer, not a payment

5.3 Operator Payment Processing

Operators handling fiat payment processing at the point of sale must maintain their own PSP arrangements and PCI-DSS compliance. These are not IX fund obligations but standard hospitality/retail regulatory requirements. The use of IXT at point of sale does not constitute a payment service provided by IX.

AML / KYC Obligations

Updated

6.1 Regulatory Basis

AML obligations for IX arise through fund management regulations, not cryptoasset regulations. The applicable framework is:

  • The Money Laundering, Terrorist Financing and Transfer of Funds (Information on the Payer) Regulations 2017 (MLR 2017), as amended
  • FATF Recommendations applicable to investment funds
  • FCA SYSC sourcebook AML systems requirements
  • Proceeds of Crime Act 2002 (POCA 2002)
Framework Change Previous analysis under crypto-specific AML guidance (JMLSG Part III) is superseded. IX's AML obligations are now governed by the fund management AML framework (JMLSG Part II — Investment Management sector guidance).

6.2 KYC at Membership Sign-Up

The IX membership model has two tiers: Explorer (free) and Patron (£30/month). A future middle "Member" tier will be introduced once 500+ Patrons exist; until then, all references in this Framework are to the two-tier model. Identity verification is required at sign-up for the paying tier. The approach is risk-based and proportionate:

TierKYC LevelVerification RequiredEDD Trigger
Explorer (Free)SimplifiedEmail verification; name; date of birthAt upgrade to Patron
Patron (£30/mo)Standard CDDGovernment-issued ID; address verification (3 months)Holdings >50,000 IXT; cash withdrawal aggregate >£2,000/quarter; or any flag from ongoing monitoring

6.3 Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

EDD is automatically triggered for:

  • All Patron tier members at onboarding
  • Any member whose IXT holdings exceed the tier-specific threshold above
  • Any member identified as a Politically Exposed Person (PEP) or connected to a sanctioned entity
  • Any redemption request exceeding £10,000 in a rolling 30-day period
  • Any unusual IXT accumulation pattern flagged by the transaction monitoring system

6.4 Fund-Level AML Reporting

As an authorised fund manager, IX must maintain:

  • A nominated Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO) — must be FCA-approved under SMCR
  • Annual MLRO report to the IX Board
  • Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) filed with the National Crime Agency (NCA) as required
  • Transaction monitoring across the IXT ecosystem, with automated alerts
  • Annual AML risk assessment of the fund and its operator components
  • Operator onboarding due diligence — Business NFT candidates must pass AML checks before certification

6.5 Record Keeping

All KYC records must be retained for a minimum of 5 years from the date of the business relationship ending. Fund transaction records must be retained for 5 years from the date of the transaction.

As fund unit holders, IXT holders benefit from the following protections:

  • Complaints process: Access to FCA-compliant complaints procedure; escalation to Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS)

IX is subject to the FCA Consumer Duty (PS22/9) as an authorised fund manager with retail clients. Key requirements:

  • Outcome 1 — Products & Services: IXT and membership tiers must be designed to meet the needs of identifiable customer segments
  • Outcome 2 — Price & Value: Membership pricing, IXT earn rates, and discounts must represent fair value
  • Outcome 3 — Consumer Understanding: Marketing and disclosure materials must be clear, fair, and not misleading
  • Outcome 4 — Consumer Support: Support channels must enable consumers to realise the benefits of their membership

7.4 Subscription and Cancellation Rights

Patron-tier members (the only paying tier) have the right to cancel their subscription at any time with 30 days notice. IXT earned during the subscription period is retained. IXT used to pay monthly subscriptions is treated as a fund unit application — no refund applies once IXT has been applied.

7.5 NAV Risk Disclosure

All IXT holders must receive clear disclosure that the NAV of IXT fluctuates and that the value of their IXT holdings may decrease as well as increase. This risk disclosure must appear prominently in all marketing materials, membership agreements, and at the point of IXT acquisition.

Employment & Tax

Major Rewrite
EMI Scheme Removed All references to Enterprise Management Incentives (EMI) schemes in v1.0 are superseded and no longer applicable. The IX employee IXT grant mechanism is now delivered exclusively through an Employee Benefit Trust (EBT) structure. This has material implications for income tax, NIC, and CGT treatment.

9.1 Employee Benefit Trust (EBT) Structure

IX operates an Employee Benefit Trust — a discretionary trust established for the benefit of eligible employees of IX and certified operator partners. The EBT holds IXT and makes discretionary grants to employees subject to the schedule below.

EBT Governance

  • Independent trustee appointed (the "EBT Trustee") — cannot be a director of IX
  • Trust deed governs grant criteria, vesting conditions, and trustee discretion
  • IXT allocated to EBT: 20% of total IXT in circulation
  • Grants made annually on employment anniversary date

9.3 Tax Treatment — IXT EBT Grants

Income Tax and PAYE

  • EBT grants are benefits in kind (BIK) — taxable as employment income at the point of vesting
  • PAYE must be operated by the employer on the cash equivalent at the date of vesting
  • Cash equivalent = NAV per IXT × number of IXT granted at the date of vesting
  • Employer Class 1A NIC applies on the BIK value; must be reported on P11D and paid by 19/22 July following tax year

Tronc Conversion

  • Where IXT is converted to cash via an operator tronc arrangement, PAYE applies on the cash equivalent
  • If the tronc is operated by an independent troncmaster (not the employer), NIC is exempt on the tronc distribution
  • The tenure bonus (5–25% additional IXT on tronc conversion) is treated as additional BIK income at the point of conversion
  • HMRC requires the troncmaster to operate PAYE independently — the employer must not instruct the troncmaster on allocation

Capital Gains Tax

  • IXT held for more than 12 months following vesting qualifies for CGT treatment on disposal (rather than income treatment) where the employee does not exercise employment-related rights
  • Business Asset Disposal Relief (BADR) may apply at the 18% CGT rate where qualifying conditions are met
  • Legal counsel must confirm BADR eligibility criteria against each employee's circumstances — this is not guaranteed
HMRC Pre-Clearance Recommended The tax treatment set out above is IX management's current understanding. Given the novel nature of fund-wrapped unit EBT grants, HMRC pre-clearance or advance agreement is strongly recommended before operating the EBT. Legal and tax counsel review is required.

Food Safety Compliance

Minor Updates

10.1 Applicable Framework

Operators holding Business NFTs in the food and beverage sector (cafes, restaurants, bars) must comply with the following food safety framework as a condition of certification:

  • Food Safety Act 1990 and Food Hygiene Regulations 2006
  • EU Regulation 852/2004 on food hygiene (retained in UK law)
  • Food Standards Agency (FSA) Code of Practice
  • Allergen regulations under the Food Information Regulations 2014 (Natasha's Law)
  • Local authority Environmental Health inspections — minimum 4-star Food Hygiene Rating required for Verified certification; 5-star required for Excellence certification

10.2 Business NFT Certification Integration

Food safety standards contribute to the Business NFT 10-pillar assessment under the Operations pillar (15% weighting) and the Supply Chain pillar (10% weighting). Operators failing food hygiene standards are subject to automatic certification review and potential decertification. See Section 15 for full Business NFT certification framework.

10.3 HACCP Requirements

All food-handling operators must maintain documented HACCP (Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points) plans. HACCP documentation is reviewed at annual Business NFT recertification. Critical control point failures are a decertification trigger under Section 15.4.

A reportable RIDDOR incident or prohibition notice from a regulatory authority constitutes a potential decertification trigger. See Section 15 for full framework.

Risk Register

Rewrite
R1 Fund Authorisation Risk — CP25/28 Approval Delay or Refusal HIGH

Description: FCA may delay or modify the CP25/28 framework, or may not grant fund manager authorisation to IX in its proposed form. Operating as a fund manager without authorisation breaches s19 FSMA 2000.

Mitigation: Engage FCA-authorised legal counsel immediately; seek pre-application meetings with FCA; do not issue IXT as fund units prior to authorisation; maintain a compliant interim structure; monitor CP25/28 consultation progress.

Owner: Fund Compliance Officer / Board

R2 NAV Calculation Methodology Challenge HIGH

Description: FCA, depositary, or investors may challenge the NAV calculation methodology — particularly the definition of "Total Distributable Profit" and the treatment of management fees and carried interest reserve.

Mitigation: Commission independent actuarial/accounting review of NAV methodology; document methodology in fund prospectus; agree methodology with depositary in advance; engage external auditor to review quarterly NAV calculations.

Owner: Fund Compliance Officer + External Auditor

R3 Business NFT Valuation Dispute MEDIUM

Description: Operators or investors may dispute the valuation of individual Business NFTs as fund components, particularly where certification tier affects IXT distribution weighting.

Mitigation: Maintain transparent, auditable 10-pillar certification methodology; independent certification reviewer; documented appeals process (see Section 15); depositary oversight of asset valuation.

Owner: Fund Compliance Officer

R4 EBT Tax Treatment Challenge by HMRC HIGH

Description: HMRC may challenge the tax treatment of EBT grants — particularly the CGT/BADR treatment for IXT held post-vesting, or the NIC exemption on tronc distributions. Potential for significant underpaid PAYE/NIC liability.

Mitigation: Seek HMRC pre-clearance before EBT operation; retain specialist employment tax counsel; ensure independent troncmaster meets HMRC independence tests; do not communicate BADR availability to employees without legal confirmation.

Owner: EBT Trustee + Legal Counsel

R5 Consumer Complaint — IXT Value / NAV Decline MEDIUM

Description: Members may complain that the value of their IXT has declined and that risk was not adequately disclosed, or that the membership fee/IXT subscription does not represent fair value under Consumer Duty.

Mitigation: Prominent NAV risk disclosure at all acquisition points; clear Consumer Duty fair value assessment documented; FCA-compliant complaints procedure; maintain FOS eligibility; regular consumer outcomes monitoring.

Owner: Fund Compliance Officer + Consumer Duty Champion

R6 Operator Food Safety Failure MEDIUM

Description: A certified Business NFT operator suffers a serious food safety incident — FSA prosecution, Food Hygiene Rating downgrade to below minimum threshold, or public food safety recall.

Mitigation: Annual HACCP review at recertification; Food Hygiene Rating below 4 is a decertification trigger; require operators to notify IX of any enforcement action within 48 hours; IX carries no direct food safety liability as fund manager.

Owner: Fund Compliance Officer (certification) / Operator (primary)

R7 Data Breach — Member or Fund Participant Data MEDIUM

Description: Unauthorised access to IXT holder, KYC, or fund transaction data — whether through cyber attack, insider threat, or third-party processor failure. Fund participant data is high-value and subject to MLR 2017 confidentiality requirements.

Mitigation: ISO 27001-aligned security controls; penetration testing; DPA agreements with all processors; breach response procedure; ICO notification within 72 hours; cyber insurance.

Owner: DPO + CTO

R8 Operator Workplace Health & Safety Incident LOW

Description: A RIDDOR-reportable incident or HSE enforcement action at a certified operator. Reputational risk to IX ecosystem; possible decertification implications.

Mitigation: Annual H&S documentation review at recertification; operators must notify IX of any RIDDOR reportable incident within 48 hours; HSE prohibition notice is an immediate decertification trigger.

Owner: Fund Compliance Officer (certification) / Operator (primary)

15.3 Annual Recertification Process

All certified operators undergo annual recertification. The process is:

  1. Notice: IX notifies operator 60 days before recertification date
  2. Evidence submission: Operator submits updated documentation for all 10 pillars within 30 days
  3. Assessment: Fund Compliance Officer conducts desk review; site visit for Excellence tier
  4. Outcome: Pass (same or upgraded tier), Conditional (30-day improvement plan), or Fail (decertification)
  5. Appeals: Operator may appeal within 14 days to an independent review panel (3 members, majority vote)

15.4 Decertification Triggers

The following events trigger an immediate certification review, with potential for emergency decertification:

  • Food Hygiene Rating below 4 stars
  • HSE prohibition notice or improvement notice
  • Financial pillar deterioration — operator unable to service fund financing (Section 17)
  • AML/KYC failure — operator fails refreshed due diligence
  • Criminal conviction or regulatory sanction against operator or its principals
  • Annual recertification score below 40% (Foundation threshold)
  • Failure to submit recertification evidence within the 30-day window

Decertification removes the operator from the fund portfolio. The Business NFT is voided. Pending IXT distributions are held pending resolution. The operator retains any IXT already vested and distributed.

Fund Management Obligations

New — v2.0

16.1 Quarterly NAV Reporting

IX is required to calculate and publish the IXT NAV within 20 business days of each quarter end. The quarterly NAV report must include:

  • Total IXT in circulation at quarter end
  • Total Distributable Profit for the quarter
  • NAV per IXT (to 4 decimal places)
  • Management fee deduction for the quarter
  • Carried interest reserve position
  • Number of certified operators contributing to distributable profit

NAV reports are published to all IXT holders via the IX member portal and to the depositary simultaneously.

16.2 Annual Fund Accounts

Annual fund accounts are prepared to FRS 102 standards (or such other standard as the FCA requires for authorised funds). The accounts must be:

  • Signed off by the IX Board and Fund Compliance Officer
  • Audited by an FCA-registered auditor
  • Reviewed and confirmed by the depositary
  • Published to all IXT holders within 4 months of fund year end
  • Filed with the FCA as required under COLL sourcebook

16.4 Carried Interest — Calculation and Distribution

The carried interest mechanism is central to the IX fund economics:

  • Hurdle Rate: 6% cumulative Preferred Return per annum (Stage 0 Founding Preferential Return is the ringfenced per-venue equivalent — see Articles v2.1 Article 34)
  • Carried Interest Rate: staged under the 5-stage waterfall — 0% during Stages 0 and 1, 10% Stage 2 (pre-payback), 20% Stage 3 (post-payback), 25% Stage 4 (above 2× MOIC)
  • Calculation period: Annual (calendar year end)
  • Catch-up: No catch-up mechanism — carried interest applies only above hurdle
  • Clawback: A clawback mechanism must be documented to address over-distribution of carried interest if subsequent years underperform

16.5 Distribution Waterfall Compliance

Priority 1 — Return of Capital
Return of committed capital to investors before any profit distribution.
Priority 2 — Preferred Return (7% p.a.)
Investors receive a 7% per annum preferred return on committed capital before carried interest is earned by IX.
Priority 3 — IX Carried Interest (20%)
IX as fund manager receives 20% of all returns above the 7% hurdle as carried interest / performance fee.
Priority 4 — Residual Distribution to IXT Holders
Remaining 80% of above-hurdle returns distributed pro-rata to IXT holders according to NAV calculation.

Financing Structure Compliance

New — v2.0

17.1 Risk Factor Framework

IX deploys a risk-adjusted interest rate framework for operator financing. The applicable risk factor is applied to the 6% base interest rate:

Operator StageRisk FactorEffective RateRationale
Pre-opening1.5x9.0%Highest risk — no trading history; pre-revenue
First year trading1.0x6.0%Moderate risk — establishing trading pattern; limited data
Established operator (12m+ certified)0.7x4.2%Lower risk — proven trading history; certified performance data

17.2 Three Financing Tiers

Tier 1 — Convertible Loan

Rate: 6% × 1.5x = 9.0% (pre-opening)

Purpose: Pre-opening capital for new Business NFT operators — fit-out, equipment, working capital.

Structure: Convertible loan note. On conversion event, outstanding principal converts to IXT at the prevailing NAV.

Conversion triggers: 12-month trading anniversary; operator election; sale of business; default.

Security: Fixed and floating charge over operator assets; personal guarantee of operator principals.

IXT conversion mechanics: Outstanding principal ÷ NAV per IXT at conversion date = IXT issued to IX fund. IXT is then distributed per the standard allocation (Operator 60% / EBT 20% / Consumer 10% / IX 10%).

Tier 2 — Cash-Flow Loan

Rate: 6% × 1.0x = 6.0% (first year)

Purpose: Working capital and growth financing for operators in their first year of certified trading.

Structure: Senior secured revolving credit facility. Not convertible — repaid in cash from operator cash flows.

Repayment: Monthly payments from operator distributable cash; sweep mechanism against excess cash.

Covenant package: Minimum DSCR of 1.2x; quarterly financial reporting; continuation of Business NFT certification.

Cross-default: Default on Cash-Flow Loan constitutes an event of default under any Convertible Loan and triggers decertification review.

Tier 3 — Equity Participation

Rate: 6% × 0.7x = 4.2% (established)

Purpose: Long-term growth capital for established, Excellence-tier certified operators.

Structure: Equity participation — IX takes a minority stake (typically 10–25%) alongside the operator. The 4.2% rate applies to any remaining loan element only.

IXT alignment: Equity participation is denominated partially in IXT. IX's equity stake entitles it to a share of operator distributable profit, which flows into the fund NAV calculation.

Exit: Operator buyout at agreed multiple; third-party sale with IX tag-along rights; IX put option at year 5.

17.3 Regulatory Disclosure Requirements

All financing arrangements between IX and operators must be disclosed in the fund's annual accounts and investor reporting. Specific disclosure requirements include:

  • Total committed capital to each operator (anonymised in public reporting)
  • Effective interest rates applied and risk factor basis
  • Outstanding loan balances at fund year end
  • Any loans in default or subject to covenant breach waiver
  • Conversion events during the year — IXT issued on conversion, NAV impact
  • Related party transactions — any operator in which IX principals hold an interest
FCA Disclosure Obligations Conflicts of interest between IX as fund manager and IX as financier of fund components must be disclosed prominently in the fund prospectus and managed through the IX Conflicts of Interest Policy. The depositary must be notified of all new financing arrangements and any material changes.

17.4 IXT Conversion Event Mechanics

On a conversion event under a Tier 1 Convertible Loan, the following process applies:

  1. Fund Compliance Officer calculates outstanding principal at conversion date
  2. Prevailing quarterly NAV per IXT is confirmed (or interim NAV calculated if mid-quarter)
  3. IXT issuance quantity calculated: Outstanding Principal ÷ NAV per IXT
  4. Newly issued IXT allocated per standard allocation: Operator 60% / EBT 20% / Consumer 10% / IX 10%
  5. Conversion notice issued to all existing IXT holders (dilution disclosure)
  6. Depositary confirms IXT issuance and NAV post-conversion
  7. Updated NAV published within 5 business days of conversion
