01Where a brand portfolio plugs in

The brand-activation network — how IX amplifies an on-trade portfolio
1 · Z-partner platform
A spirits group becomes a Z partner of IX. White-label member layer under the brand. Management-fee model (3–8% of gross venue revenue depending on tier), no upfront platform cost. Founding Z slots ramp 1/2/3% across Y1–Y3. Five slots; one taken (Vinicon); four open.
2 · Venue network
~15 EU cities pipeline. UKIYO London live as first certified venue. Every new IX-certified operator is a new pourable venue for the partner's portfolio. Vetted on the 10-pillar IX Certification (Foundation / Verified / Excellence) — so the brand association is clean.
3 · Member loyalty engine
Patron-tier members (£30/mo) earn IXT (1 IXT = £1 floor) on qualifying spend. Brand-led activations — tasting events, pairing dinners, education series, allocation drops — can be Patron-only or open. IXT redemption flows back to the venue, not lost as breakage.
4 · Brand ambassador role inside IX
Brand ambassadors are the most under-leveraged asset in on-trade brand work — the relationships, the trade education, the venue introductions. IX gives that role a structural place: trade-side member tier (or staff EBT participation), event hosting rights at certified venues, IXT-funded sampling/pairing budgets that compound into ecosystem value rather than disappearing.
Vinicon as anchor. Vinicon is the first signed Z partner — wine agency, on-trade distribution, founding-rate economics. Their wine list flows into the IX certified-venue network; their customers flow into Patron membership. Rémy's portfolio (Louis XIII, Rémy Martin, Cointreau, Mount Gay, Bruichladdich, etc.) is a direct analogue at higher-LTV spirits scale. Same template applies.
The bet. Independent hospitality is fragmented and brand activation is expensive. A network that consolidates the venue + member layer makes brand work in on-trade dramatically more efficient: one Z-partner agreement reaches every IX-certified venue, members opt in to brand-led experiences via the loyalty engine, and the IXT floor means activation budgets generate compounding ecosystem value rather than disappearing into discount breakage.

04The certified venue network (where Rémy's portfolio would land)

Every venue in the network is scored on the 10-pillar IX Certification: Operations, Sustainability, Financial Health, People & HR, Customer Experience, Supply Chain, Governance, Community, Growth, Economic Participation. Three certification tiers based on aggregate score: Foundation (40%+), Verified (60%+), Excellence (80%+). Score decay if standards slip; quarterly reassessment.

For a luxury spirits group the relevant filter is the Verified+ pool — venues operating at the standard where a Louis XIII allocation makes sense. UKIYO is the first live venue. The 15-EU-cities pipeline is being assembled now.

05Regulatory posture

Two-entity split. InitiativeX Ltd (unregulated) handles the consumer membership, IXT loyalty earning/spending, B2B platform, and management fees. Appointed FCA-authorised fund manager handles all six investment vehicles, IXT cash withdrawal (where it triggers e-money), financial promotions, and client money.

For a Z-partner relationship specifically, the regulated activity sits with the fund manager — the brand partner only interacts with the unregulated platform layer (member management, IXT loyalty, white-label venue network). FCA Opinion Letter v1.1 is with counsel.

Full FCA letter and the IX Regulatory & Compliance Framework v2.1 are linked in §08 below — both confidential / counsel-side.
