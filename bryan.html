04What I'd specifically value from your first overall review

Four asks, in order of usefulness

1. Corporate-side gating before FCA-specialist counsel. Reading the FCA letter first — what corporate decisions in the Articles, Shareholders Register or EBT Trust Deed need to be locked in or amended before a regulatory specialist can give a clean opinion? I'd rather fix the corporate plumbing now than have FCA counsel come back saying the corporate structure isn't tight enough to opine on.

2. Risk register from a VC/PE drafting lens. Where would Fox Williams' corporate practice flag exposure I haven't flagged myself? Specifically: (a) the P-share waterfall mechanics in Articles 21 & 34 against standard UK VC convention — is the Stage 0 per-venue ringfencing defensible or does it look unusual enough to spook a sophisticated LP? (b) The G-share MIP in Article 35 against typical Seed/Series A vesting conventions — four-year vest with one-year cliff is standard, but the IRR-tiered participation rate (5/10/15/20% by MOIC band) is more PE than VC. (c) The IXT minting framework in Articles 27–30 + Board Resolution R-08 — how do you handle drafting a share-class-adjacent instrument that's deliberately not a share?

3. Specialist counsel recommendation. Who does Fox Williams suggest for the FCA-specialist opinion? I have a shortlist but no commitment. If there's a firm or partner you'd specifically introduce or specifically warn off, that signal matters.

4. Engagement structure. How would you prefer to scope this — fixed-fee first-overall-review now, then matter-by-matter as we go, or a broader retainer-style arrangement once the FCA opinion is back? Whatever's normal for your engagement model.
