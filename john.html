5-stage waterfall. Stage 0 — Founding investors get 6% p.a. cumulative preferred (per venue, ringfenced from venue cashflow only). Stage 1 — pari passu return of capital, 0% carry. Stage 2 — 10% carry pre-payback. Stage 3 — 20% post-payback. Stage 4 — 25% above 2× MOIC.

04Valuation methodology — how the assets are marked

Each Business NFT (the per-venue underlying) is valued under the IX Business NFT Valuation Methodology v2.0: stage-dependent DCF with WACC built from four additive layers (base + stage premium R5→R1 + certification premium F/V/E + governance premium G1→G5), capex floor, explicit debt deduction, and lease renewal probability adjusting the terminal value. Quarterly reassessment.

Fund NAV rolls up from the aggregate Business NFT valuations + the loan books (Cashflow Notes + Development Convertibles + Participation Notes + PropCo Notes). Quarterly published.

References standard UK hospitality M&A practice (Christie & Co / Davis Coffer Lyons / Fleurets all use the same DCF + capex floor + lease probability model). Not the deprecated score-×-multiple-×-revenue formula that some earlier IX docs referenced.

05Regulatory posture

Two-entity split. InitiativeX Ltd (unregulated) handles the consumer membership, IXT loyalty earning/spending, B2B platform, and management fees. Appointed FCA-authorised fund manager handles all six investment vehicles, IXT cash withdrawal (where it triggers e-money), financial promotions, and client money.

FCA Opinion Instruction Letter v1.1 (21 questions) is with counsel. Covers: IXT classification, CIS/AIFMD, EMI/EBT, financial promotions, phased launch, six-vehicle treatment, two-layer retail-access model, staff-investor MAR exposure, FPO Art.25 introducer fees, per-venue founding preferential return, multi-entity Brand Holdco / OpCo / PropCo structure.

If the regulatory posture is the bit you find most interesting, the full FCA letter and the IX Regulatory & Compliance Framework v2.1 are sharable on request — both confidential / counsel-side.
