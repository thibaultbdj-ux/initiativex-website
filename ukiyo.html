The Story

A hand roll bar that became a proving ground

UKIYO opened in the heart of Covent Garden with a simple idea: serve exceptional Japanese hand rolls at the counter, in a space that felt intimate and intentional. No conveyor belts. No fusion gimmicks. Just precise preparation, quality ingredients, and a real connection between the person making your food and the person eating it.

It became the first venue to be certified under the IX framework — not because it was designed that way, but because it already embodied the principles. When we assessed UKIYO against all 10 pillars, it scored across every dimension. That's when we knew the model could work.

We didn't build UKIYO to prove a theory. We built it because we believed in the format. The certification came after — and it validated everything.
Year One Performance

The numbers behind the model

UKIYO's first full financial year (March 2025 – February 2026) demonstrated that a small, focused, independently operated venue can generate serious revenue while maintaining strong margins.

At £1.4M in revenue from a single 40-cover site, UKIYO outperformed most comparable concepts in central London. The 69% gross profit margin sits well above the industry average of 60–65% for similar formats.

Audited figures from an operating business, not projections. This is what quality operations look like when the infrastructure supports them.
