01The three lanes — how an SP fits in IX

Lane 1
Operator-side
Each IX-certified venue is scored on the 10-pillar IX Certification. Pillars 02 (Sustainability), 05 (Customer Experience), 06 (Supply Chain) all touch beverage programme work directly. A consultant operating to the IX standard improves the venue's score — visible to the network, audit-trail-backed.
Lane 2 · primary
SP Network
Pre-qualified leads (every newly-certified venue is a brief that needs filling), group-procurement scale across the certified base, IXT-denominated payment option. Founding SP slots free during Phase 1; no exclusivity required.
Lane 3
Supplier-side
Drinks brands, distributors, equipment partners can join the IX certified-supplier programme. As a consultant, the SP sits between operators and suppliers — IX gives a structural place to recommend in either direction, scored and visible.

04Why the SP role compounds inside IX (vs project-by-project freelance)

The traditional consulting model is project-by-project: define the programme, hand it over, move on. No compounding upside for the consultant beyond a referral chain. Inside IX:

  • Network-visible scoring. Performance is documented and visible — competitive moat that grows with the network
  • Compounding pipeline. Every newly-certified venue is automatically a brief surface — TAM grows passively as IX onboards venues
  • IXT position. 50% IXT election creates a network stake that grows with the ecosystem rather than cashing out per-engagement
  • Scored impact on venues. The consultant's work materially moves Pillar scores → R-rating → WACC Governance Premium → Business NFT valuation. Documented contribution beyond the engagement fee.
  • Brief curation cost = zero. No more sales effort — qualified briefs surface automatically
